Why Age Adjusted Death Rate?
All datapoints use age adjusted death rate per 100,000 people. Age majorly affects the risk of death, as older people are more likely to get sick. This is why age-adjusted death rates can help make better comparisons between different populations, or mortality over time within one area as the population changes.

Notice:
The "New York City Leading Causes of Death" data set had its 2019 data removed. Previously, 2019 was the most recent year in that set. Additionally, there existed a 5 year gap between 2019 and 2014. This means that, as of 12/12/22, there is no data for 2015 onward. The Top 5 and Demographic Comparison charts reflect this issue.
